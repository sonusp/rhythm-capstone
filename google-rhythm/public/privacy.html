Privacy Policy for Rhythm

Last Updated: June 15, 2026

Rhythm ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our application.

1. The "Zero-Knowledge" Architecture

Rhythm is built on a strict "Zero-Knowledge" privacy model. We do not have a central database that stores your personal health data. All of your health logs, symptoms, and cycle data are stored locally on your device and are end-to-end encrypted before being backed up.

2. Use of Google Workspace APIs

Our application requests access to your Google Account to provide secure cloud backups. Rhythm's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

3. What Data We Collect and Why

  • Google Account Email & Name: We collect your email address and name solely to create a unique identifier for your backup recovery PIN in our secure Firebase infrastructure.
  • Google Drive AppData Folder: We request the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.appdata scope. We use this exclusively to create a hidden, encrypted backup file (google_rhythm_backup.json) in your personal Google Drive. We cannot see, read, or access any of your other personal files in Google Drive.
  • Health Data: Your health logs are never sent to our servers in plaintext. They are encrypted using an AES-GCM cipher with a PIN known only to you before being uploaded to your Drive.

4. Data Sharing and Selling

We do not sell your data. We do not share your personal information or health data with third parties, advertisers, or analytics companies.

5. Data Retention and Deletion

You have full control over your data. You can delete your local data at any time by clearing your browser storage. You can delete your encrypted Google Drive backup by navigating to your Google Drive settings, selecting "Manage Apps," and deleting the hidden app data for Rhythm.

6. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact the developer directly or open an issue on the project repository.
